Global Health Data Science Analysis

Modeling the Determinants of Population Health (2012–2021)

Academic Capstone – Langara College, 2024 Python R Data Visualization
Project Overview

This project leverages modern data science techniques to analyze longitudinal global health indicators, environmental metrics, and socioeconomic factors from 2012 to 2021. My aim was to uncover patterns, quantify relationships, and generate actionable insights for evidence-based public health strategies.

  • Multi-year trend analysis of population health indicators
  • Quantification of environmental and economic impacts on health outcomes
  • Predictive modeling for at-risk region identification
  • Actionable, data-driven recommendations

Methodology
Data Science Pipeline
  • Data Acquisition & Cleaning: Sourced, validated, and standardized dataset
  • Exploratory Analysis: Visualized trends, assessed distributions, identified outliers
  • Feature Engineering: Constructed derived variables, regional aggregations
  • Statistical Modeling: Time series decomposition, logistic regression, correlation/multivariate analysis
  • Validation & Interpretation: Model assessment, coefficient interpretation, and public health context
Key Findings & Insights
Regional Inequities

Persistent disparities in health outcomes, especially in Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia, highlight critical intervention needs.

Environmental Drivers

Environmental quality—especially air and water—emerged as strong predictors of population health, with significant effects in urban contexts.

Socioeconomic Leverage

Education and GDP per capita were robust predictors of improved health; threshold effects noted in certain regions.

Temporal Shifts

While global health outcomes improved over time, progress rates vary greatly by region and indicator.

Predictive Power

Advanced models successfully identified at-risk regions, supporting targeted intervention strategies.
